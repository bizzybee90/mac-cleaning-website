How Often Should You Get Your Windows Cleaned?

20 March 2026 · 6 min read

The Short Answer

Around 70% of our customers choose 4-weekly cleaning. It keeps windows consistently clear, costs less per visit, and means your home always looks its best. That said, 8-weekly works well for certain homes too — and we’ll explain exactly when below.

There’s no single right answer. It depends on where you live, what surrounds your property, and how much window grime bothers you. Here’s what we’ve learned from cleaning 840+ homes across Bedfordshire.

4-Weekly Cleaning: The Most Popular Choice

Four weeks is the sweet spot for most homes in the Luton and Bedfordshire area. Here’s why it works so well:

  • Windows never build up heavy grime. Because we’re back before dirt really takes hold, each clean is quick and thorough. The pure water system works best when it’s maintaining clean glass rather than battling months of buildup.
  • It costs less per clean. Less dirt means less time on each visit, so the per-clean price is lower than 8-weekly. We pass that saving straight on.
  • Your home always looks presentable. Whether it’s the in-laws visiting or you’re just pulling into the drive after work, the windows look good all month.
  • Frames and sills stay cleaner too. Regular cleaning stops mould, algae, and cobwebs from getting established on your uPVC frames.

4-weekly is ideal if: you live near a busy road (think the A6 through Luton, or anywhere along the M1 corridor), you have kids leaving fingerprints on patio doors every weekend, your property faces open fields that kick up dust, or you simply like things looking sharp.

8-Weekly Cleaning: When It Makes Sense

Eight-weekly isn’t a compromise — for certain homes, it’s genuinely the right call.

  • Sheltered properties. If your house is set back from the road, surrounded by fencing or other buildings, and doesn’t catch much wind-blown dirt, windows stay cleaner for longer.
  • Quiet streets and cul-de-sacs. Less traffic means less dust settling on glass. Some of our customers in villages like Barton-le-Clay and Slip End find 8-weekly is plenty.
  • Second homes or rental properties. If you’re not there every day, 8-weekly keeps things maintained without overspending.
  • Tighter budgets. The yearly total is lower on 8-weekly, even though each individual clean costs a bit more.

The trade-off is straightforward: windows pick up 8 weeks of dirt, dust, cobwebs, and water marks between visits, so each clean takes longer and costs more per session. You’ll also notice windows looking tired in weeks 5–8, especially after rain.

One thing worth knowing: switching between 4-weekly and 8-weekly is easy. Just drop us a message and we’ll adjust your schedule from the next visit. No contracts, no fuss.

What Affects How Quickly Your Windows Get Dirty?

Bedfordshire has some specific factors that speed up how fast your glass gets grubby. We see these come up time and again:

  • Proximity to the M1 or A6. Road dust and brake particles carry further than you’d think. If you can hear motorway traffic from your garden, your windows are collecting that grime daily.
  • Luton Airport flight path. Jet fuel residue is a real thing. Homes under the flight path — particularly across Luton, Stopsley, and parts of Hitchin — develop a fine oily film on south-facing glass.
  • Nearby construction. With the Luton town centre regeneration, Power Court development, and new builds spreading around Houghton Regis, construction dust has been a constant issue for nearby homes over the past couple of years.
  • Trees and hedges close to windows. Pollen coats everything in spring. Autumn brings leaf tannin stains and sap spots. If you’ve got mature trees within a few metres of the house, 4-weekly is almost always the better shout.
  • North-facing vs south-facing. North-facing windows stay damp longer and grow green algae and biofilm faster. South-facing glass dries quicker but shows water spots and dust more visibly.
  • Hard water area. Bedfordshire sits on chalk, which means our tap water is hard. When rain picks up chalky dust from paths and roofs, it leaves stubborn white water spots as it dries on glass. Regular cleaning stops these from etching in.

If three or more of those apply to your home, 4-weekly will make a noticeable difference to how your windows look day-to-day.

So yes, 8-weekly saves around £120 a year on a typical 3-bed. But there’s a catch: your windows are only truly “fresh” for the first couple of weeks after each clean. For the remaining 6 weeks, dirt is building back up. With 4-weekly, that window (no pun intended) of clean glass covers most of the month.

You can switch frequency at any time. Some customers go 4-weekly through spring and summer when pollen and dust are worst, then drop to 8-weekly over winter. Whatever works for you.

What We Recommend

Start with 4-weekly. It’s what works best for the majority of homes we clean across Luton, Dunstable, Harpenden, and the surrounding villages. You’ll see the difference immediately, and because each clean is lighter, the cost per visit stays low.

If after a few months you feel your windows are holding up well between visits, switch to 8-weekly. We won’t try to talk you out of it — some homes genuinely don’t need the extra frequency.

Either way, there are no contracts. You can change your schedule, pause for a holiday, or cancel any time with a quick message. We keep things simple because that’s how we’d want it ourselves.

The best frequency is whichever one you’ll actually stick with. Regular cleaning — at any interval — beats a one-off deep clean every six months. Your windows will look better, last longer, and your home will feel that bit sharper every time you walk up to the front door.
